Our Story

Built by operators,
for operators.

We didn't build MortuPro because we saw a market opportunity. We built it because we needed it ourselves — and nothing else existed.

The Origin

We are the customer.

STAT MCS is a mortuary transport company based in Southeast Texas. We handle removals for funeral homes, crematories, and medical facilities across the region — 24 hours a day, 7 days a week.

When we started, we used a combination of group texts, spreadsheets, and manual invoicing to run the operation. It worked — until it didn't. Missed calls at 3 AM. Invoices never sent. Drivers who didn't know the pickup address until the last minute.

We looked for software. We tried what was available. Most tools were built for funeral homes, not transport companies. The ones that existed were outdated, clunky, or priced for enterprise customers.

So we built what we wished existed. That software became MortuPro.

Today, STAT MCS runs on MortuPro for every single transport. We are the first customer, the most demanding tester, and the loudest advocate. Every feature you see is something we use ourselves.

How we got here

🚐

STAT MCS founded

Tommy Morgado starts FCR as a mortuary transport company in Southeast Texas. Running on group texts and spreadsheets. Works well enough — for now.

💡

The problem becomes obvious

A missed call at 2 AM. An invoice never sent. A driver who navigated to the wrong hospital. It's clear the operation needs real software — and nothing on the market fits.

🔧

First internal version built

We build a basic dispatch system just for FCR. Smart intake, SMS alerts, a driver mobile link. It's rough but it works. The team loves it immediately.

🚀

MortuPro launches publicly

After refining the platform on FCR's real operations for months, we open it to other mortuary transport companies. The response validates everything.

📈

127 operations and counting
